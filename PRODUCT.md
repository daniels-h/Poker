# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A private poker club. Two audiences: an admin (one or few people) who records session results, manages players, and edits data; and general club members who view-only — checking the leaderboard, their stats, past sessions. Members are relaxed and social, checking in after a game night to see who's up and who's down. The admin is task-focused, entering results quickly after a session.

## Product Purpose

Track poker club results over time. Sessions, buy-ins, cashouts, net P&L per player. Surfaces leaderboards, session history, player profiles, and streaks. Success = members enjoy checking their standing and the club has a reliable record of every game.

## Brand Personality

Fun, club-like, sharp. Not a tech product — a club's record book. The tone is insider, a little irreverent, and proud of its members. Think a sports club bulletin board that got a very good designer.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-generated SaaS dashboards (shadcn defaults, soft rounded cards, purple/blue gradients, glassmorphism)
- Crypto trading apps (neon on black, aggressive red/green)
- Corporate BI dashboards (sterile, gray, enterprise)
- Generic casino/gambling sites (kitsch, tacky gold, flashing elements)

## Design Principles

1. **The club, not the software.** Every screen should feel like it belongs to this specific group of people, not like a generic tool they happen to use.
2. **Numbers are the hero.** P&L figures, win rates, streaks — these are what members come to see. Typography and layout should make them impossible to miss.
3. **Earned personality.** Personality comes from committed typographic and color choices, not decorative flourishes. No borders-for-the-sake-of-borders, no icons-as-decoration.
4. **Density without clutter.** Club members want to scan results fast. Information should be dense but breathe — tight within groups, generous between them.
5. **Familiar structure, unfamiliar finish.** Standard nav and table patterns (members know how to use them) finished with a strong, distinctive visual identity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort. No specific requirements beyond readable contrast and usable tap targets.
